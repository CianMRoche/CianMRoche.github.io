How does this constrain dark matter? In cold dark matter (CDM), clusters have cuspy, steep central potentials — the BCG oscillates rapidly over a small range. Self-interacting dark matter softens the core, flattening the potential and allowing wider, slower oscillations. Measuring BCG–centre offsets across many clusters constrains the self-interaction cross-section σ/m. The oscillation speed and amplitude here are physically tied to the displayed potential via energy conservation.
