How does this constrain dark matter? In cold dark matter (CDM), clusters have cuspy central potentials — the BCG is confined to a small region near the center. Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) softens the core, allowing the BCG to wander further. Measuring BCG–center offsets across many clusters constrains the self-interaction cross-section σ/m.
