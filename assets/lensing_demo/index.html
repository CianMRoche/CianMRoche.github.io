Physics: For each output pixel at angular position θ, the shader solves the lens equation β = θ − α(θ) to find the source-plane position β, then samples the source image there (inverse ray tracing). SIS deflection: α = θ_E · (θ−θ_L)/|θ−θ_L|. NFW adds a softening core radius. EPL applies an elliptical coordinate transform. Magnification follows from the Jacobian of β(θ) — bright arcs appear where the mapping is nearly singular (caustic crossings).
